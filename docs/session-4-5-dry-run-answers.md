## Session 4 Dry Run: Signal Reviewer

Candidate: `Session 4 candidate`

Segment: `Signal reviewer`

1. Does the Signal Trust Panel explain why this signal appeared now?

```text
Yes. The panel makes the signal easier to understand because it connects the trade idea to the rationale, confidence drivers, and risk setup. I can see more than just a buy or sell label, which helps me understand why the signal appeared now.
```

2. Does the invalidation condition make the setup easier to trust?

```text
Yes. The invalidation condition is important because it tells me what would prove the signal wrong. That makes the setup feel more disciplined and less like a black-box prediction.
```

3. Are the confidence drivers enough, or do you still need proof?

```text
The confidence drivers help, but I still need more proof before trusting it fully. I would want recent price-action context, model history, or a short audit trail showing why the signal passed.
```

4. Would you place a tiny paper trade from this view?

```text
Yes, I would place a tiny paper trade if the app clearly shows paper mode, entry, stop, target, estimated notional, and risk. I would not use live money yet.
```

5. What would make this worth paying for?

```text
It would be worth paying for if it saves review time, explains why a signal passed, tracks paper-trade results, and helps avoid emotional trades.
```

Practice score:

```text
Trust score: 4
Value score: 4
Would pay: Maybe
Friction: Needs more proof, recent price-action context, and audit history.
Notes: The trust panel improves clarity, but the next trust unlock is evidence that the signal logic works repeatedly.
```

```text
Yes. Seeing PAPER clearly reduces anxiety because I know the order is not using real money. It makes me more willing to test the workflow.
```

2. Do estimated notional and risk per unit make the trade feel controlled?

```text
Yes. Estimated notional and risk per unit make the trade feel controlled because I can understand exposure before clicking.
```

```text
Mostly yes. The setup is understandable, but I would still want one final confirmation screen showing quantity, notional, stop, target, and maximum loss before the order is sent.
```

```text
Yes, I would place a tiny Alpaca paper trade from this view. I would stay paper-only until there is more trade history, audit trail clarity, and stronger risk controls.
```

5. What would make this worth paying for?

```text
It would be worth paying for if it combines safe paper execution, performance tracking, signal explanations, risk controls, and a clear report of what worked and what failed.
```

Practice score:

```text
Trust score: 4
Value score: 5
Would pay: Maybe
Friction: Wants final pre-submit confirmation, maximum-loss display, and order audit trail.
Notes: Paper mode and risk fields make the workflow feel safer. A final trade confirmation would increase execution trust.
```
