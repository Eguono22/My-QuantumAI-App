# Emmanuel Session 1 Brief

Use this for the first live beta call with Emmanuel.

## Candidate

- Name: `Emmanuel`
- Segment: `MT5 trader`
- Status: `SCHEDULED`
- Focus: `trust in setup, signal review, risk visibility, and paper-trade confidence`

## Goal

Learn whether Emmanuel finds the QuantumAI paper-trading workflow trustworthy enough to use again.

## Session Length

`30 minutes`

## Before The Call

1. Make sure the local or live app is already running.
2. Open `/app/pilot`.
3. Open the main product areas you may need:
   - `/app/connect`
   - `/app/signals`
   - any risk/order/portfolio screens used in the normal flow
4. Keep the feedback form ready so you can save notes before the call ends.
5. Keep [`docs/emmanuel-session-1-live-notes.md`](./emmanuel-session-1-live-notes.md) open during the call.

## Call Structure

### 1. Current Workflow

Ask:

```text
What do you currently use to decide whether a signal is worth acting on?
```

What to listen for:

- whether he already uses MT5 alerts, manual chart review, Discord/Telegram signals, or prop-style rules
- what makes him trust or ignore a trade setup

### 2. Setup / Connection Confidence

Ask him to inspect the setup or connection flow without taking over.

What to watch:

- where he pauses
- whether terms are unclear
- whether he knows what is safe vs paper-only

### 3. Signal Review

Ask him to review one AI-generated signal.

Ask:

```text
What here makes you trust this signal, and what still feels unclear?
```

What to watch:

- whether rationale is understandable
- whether entry, stop, target, and confidence feel actionable
- whether anything feels hand-wavy or unsafe

### 4. Risk Controls

Before any paper trade, ask him to inspect the visible risk controls.

Ask:

```text
What information would you need before placing even a tiny paper trade?
```

What to watch:

- whether he notices max notional, daily limits, or risk-per-trade guidance
- whether the system feels protective or confusing

### 5. Value Test

Ask:

```text
What would make this worth paying for?
```

What to listen for:

- time saved
- better discipline
- clearer rationale
- better trade filtering
- anything he says is missing from the core loop
